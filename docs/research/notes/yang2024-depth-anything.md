# Setting

**Problem class**: Zero-shot monocular depth estimation (MDE) — predict relative depth from a single RGB image without test-domain supervision.

**Inputs**: single RGB image, arbitrary resolution (shorter side resized to 518 during training; inference pads both sides to multiples of 14 for DINOv2 patch compatibility). No prior on camera intrinsics or scene scale.

**Outputs**: dense affine-invariant depth map (disparity-space, normalized per-image); no absolute scale guarantee from the base model. A separate fine-tuning step onto NYUv2 or KITTI recovers metric scale for indoor / outdoor scenes respectively.

**Key precondition**: depth values are first mapped to disparity space (d = 1/t) and then normalized per sample to remove unknown scale and shift across heterogeneous training datasets (§3.1).

# Core idea

Depth Anything is a data-scaling thesis: robust monocular depth comes from data coverage, not from novel architectures. The method reuses the MiDaS affine-invariant loss (Eq. 1–3) and DPT decoder, but augments the 1.5M-image labeled set with 62M pseudo-labeled unlabeled images generated by a teacher model (Eq. 4). Two refinements make the unlabeled data useful: (1) **strong perturbations** (CutMix Eq. 5–8 + color jitter + Gaussian blur) force the student to learn beyond imitating the teacher, and (2) a **feature alignment loss** (Eq. 9) keeps the student's encoder close to a frozen DINOv2 encoder, preserving DINOv2's semantic priors. The backbone is a DINOv2-initialized ViT encoder (S/B/L), giving the model a powerful visual foundation before MDE training begins.

The result is a relative-depth foundation model that transfers zero-shot to unseen domains (KITTI, NYUv2, Sintel, DDAD, ETH3D, DIODE) and, after metric fine-tuning, sets new SOTA on NYUv2 (δ1=0.984) and KITTI (δ1=0.982).

# Assumptions

1. **Disparity linearises depth**: depths are converted to disparity d=1/t before loss computation, which assumes a perspective camera model (hard; fails for orthographic or unusual optics).
2. **Affine ambiguity is acceptable**: the base model produces relative depth up to per-image scale and shift; downstream applications that require absolute scale must fine-tune with metric data (soft for relative use-cases; hard for robotics/AR without fine-tuning).
3. **Teacher quality sufficient for pseudo-labels**: the initial MiDaS-style teacher (ViT-L, trained on 1.5M labeled images) produces dense depth maps reliable enough for the student to improve from — this holds empirically but collapses in domains completely absent from the labeled set (hard failure mode).
4. **DINOv2 features are semantically rich**: the feature alignment loss assumes DINOv2's frozen features encode scene semantics better than discrete segmentation masks; violated if DINOv2 fails on a domain (e.g. heavily synthetic or medical imagery).
5. **Patch size 14**: training crops must be multiples of 14 to match DINOv2's ViT patch size; inference images are padded accordingly (hard; other patch sizes require a different backbone).
6. **Sky has maximum depth**: a pre-trained semantic segmentation model (SegFormer [70]) detects sky and forces its disparity to 0 (farthest) — assumes sky is always the farthest object (soft; fails for aerial imagery looking down).

# Failure regime

- **No metric scale from base model**: zero-shot metric depth on held-out domains (Table 5) shows significant error on some — SUN RGB-D AbsRel=0.500, DIODE Outdoor AbsRel=0.794. The relative-depth output requires metric fine-tuning for any scale-sensitive application.
- **Naïve self-training fails**: adding unlabeled images without strong perturbations does not improve over the labeled-only baseline (Table 9, rows 1 vs 2: mean AbsRel 0.180 vs 0.180). The student simply imitates the teacher and learns no new knowledge.
- **Semantically similar but depth-varying regions**: DINOv2 produces similar features for different parts of the same object (e.g. car front and rear) that have different depths. Without the tolerance margin α, enforcing identical features hurts depth discrimination (Table 12: α=1.0 gives mean AbsRel 0.188 vs 0.175 at α=0.85).
- **Feature alignment on labeled data is neutral or harmful**: applying L_feat to labeled images does not help (Table 13, "L" column), presumably because high-quality manual labels are already informative enough without the auxiliary semantic constraint.
- **MegaDepth specialises on ultra-remote scenes**: the MegaDepth dataset alone generalises poorly to the six standard evaluation domains (Table 6, AbsRel 0.274 vs ensemble 0.180) — diversity of scene geometry within a dataset matters more than raw image count.

# Numerical sensitivity

- **Normalization**: t(d) = median(d), s(d) = (1/HW) Σ|dᵢ − t(d)| (Eq. 3). This is a median-based scale–shift normalisation. The MAD-like scale is sensitive to outliers in sparse ground truth; maps with large textureless background (e.g. sky in MegaDepth) are explicitly handled by zeroing sky disparity before loss.
- **Tolerance margin α**: ablated in Table 12. α=0.85 is optimal; α=1.00 (no margin) raises mean AbsRel by 0.013; α=0.70 is slightly worse than 0.85. This hyperparameter is not robust to large distribution shift — should be re-tuned if adapting to a new domain.
- **Learning rate asymmetry**: encoder LR = 5e-6, decoder LR = 5e-5 (10× larger). When fine-tuning to metric depth (ZoeDepth codebase), the ratio increases to 1:50 (encoder 1/50 of decoder) because the Depth Anything encoder is a much stronger initialisation than the MiDaS encoder.
- **Patch constraint at inference**: both image dimensions must be multiples of 14; the paper pads to the nearest multiple and evaluates at original resolution by interpolating predictions. Failure to pad causes misaligned feature grids.
- **CutMix 50% probability**: applied stochastically; reducing probability weakens the perturbation effect (not ablated directly, but the binary on/off ablation in Table 9 shows S is critical).

# Applicability

- **Use when**: need generalizable, robust relative depth for in-the-wild images; when the target domain lacks labeled depth; when initialising a metric-depth model (replace MiDaS encoder with Depth Anything encoder in ZoeDepth).
- **Don't use when**: absolute metric depth is required out of the box without any fine-tuning; when operating on imagery very far from the eight unlabeled source domains (satellite, medical, microscopy); when real-time inference on mobile is required (ViT-L is 335M params; ViT-S at 24.8M is the smallest option).
- **Compared against**: MiDaS v3.1 (DPT-BEiTL-512, same problem class — Depth Anything ViT-L improves KITTI AbsRel from 0.127 → 0.076, NYU from 0.048 → 0.043); ZoeDepth (metric-depth extension of MiDaS — Depth Anything encoder drop-in improves ZoeDepth on zero-shot metric benchmarks, Table 5).

- **Motivation**: Scale unlabeled data to build a depth foundation model — MiDaS was bottlenecked by labeled dataset coverage; 62M unlabeled images provide breadth without sensor or stereo cost.
- **Architecture**: DINOv2 ViT encoder (ViT-S 24.8M / ViT-B 97.5M / ViT-L 335.3M params) + DPT decoder; training resolution 518×518; patch size 14; depth computed in disparity space (d = 1/t).
- **Training — data engine**: two-stage self-training. Stage 1: teacher trained on 1.5M labeled images (BlendedMVS, DIML, HRWSI, IRS, MegaDepth, TartanAir) for 20 epochs using affine-invariant loss. Stage 2: teacher (ViT-L) pseudo-labels 62M unlabeled images from 8 datasets (SA-1B, Open Images, ImageNet-21K, BDD100K, LSUN, Objects365, Places365, Google Landmarks); student trained on labeled + pseudo-labeled at 1:2 ratio.
- **Training — CutMix challenge**: unlabeled images fed to student are strongly perturbed (color jitter, Gaussian blur, CutMix at 50% probability); images fed to teacher for pseudo-labeling are clean. The split loss (Eq. 7–8) supervises masked regions M and 1−M separately.
- **Training — DINOv2 feature alignment**: auxiliary loss L_feat = 1 − (1/HW) Σ cos(fᵢ, fᵢ') (Eq. 9) aligns student encoder features with a frozen DINOv2 encoder; pixels where cosine similarity already exceeds α=0.85 are excluded to avoid over-constraining depth-varying regions within an object. Applied to unlabeled data only.
- **Training — affine-invariant loss**: inherited from MiDaS [ranftl2019-midas]; normalises each depth map by median (shift) and mean absolute deviation from median (scale) before computing L1 (Eq. 1–3). Total loss = average of L_l + L_u + L_feat.

- **Assessment**: data-scaling thesis validated — 62M unlabeled images improve zero-shot generalisation substantially when combined with perturbation + semantic alignment. The encoder serves as a drop-in replacement for MiDaS in ZoeDepth metric-depth pipelines. Superseded for practical use by Depth Anything V2 [yang2024-depth-anything-v2], which improves backbone and training data quality; V1 is retained as the lineage anchor.
